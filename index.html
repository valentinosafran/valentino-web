About

I am a PhD student and researcher at the HUMADEX group, University of Maribor (UM FERI), specializing in telecommunications, digital health transformation, IoT and cybersecurity. With a Master’s in Telecommunications, I am actively involved in European research projects, focusing on integrating AI, microservices and advanced networking technologies to enhance healthcare systems and overall quality of life.

I have hands-on experience in developing and deploying microservices, cloud infrastructures, and working with key technologies, including Python, Java, Spring Boot, REST, MQTT, Apache Kafka, Keycloak, and Linux-based systems. I also specialize in HL7 FHIR for healthcare data interoperability, virtualization with VMs and containers. I'm activly using LLMs like ChatGPT, Perplexity and Gemini for intelligent automation. My research focuses on AI-driven digital health transformation, cybersecurity and the integration of emerging technologies to enhance system resilience and efficiency.

Work Experience

UM FERI
2020 - Present

As a member of the Laboratory for Digital Signal Processing and the HUMADEX group, I have worked on multiple European projects, including PERSIST (H2020), HOSMARTAI (H2020), PALANTIR (H2020), SMILE (Horizon), and AI4HOPE (Horizon). My expertise lies in developing and deploying microservices, cloud infrastructures, AI-driven solutions, and secure healthcare platforms.

PERSIST (H2020)

I contributed to the development of an advanced multimodal sensing network for data transmission and access to microservices, aimed at assessing individuals' quality of life. My role involved:

  • Infrastructure & Hardware: Managing and maintaining physical servers forming the project's multimodal sensing network.
  • Software Development: Working with virtual machines, microservices, and containers using Python, Flask, Spring Boot, Apache Camel, and Kafka.
  • Data Communication: Implementing MQTT and HTTP (REST) protocols to support message queuing and event streaming architectures.

HOSMARTAI (H2020)

My work focused on developing an FHIR server and a patient health resource database based on the HL7 FHIR standard. Key responsibilities included:

  • Setting up and managing the hardware infrastructure at UM FERI.
  • Developing and integrating Python and Java-based microservices using RESTful architecture to enhance AI-driven healthcare transformation.
  • Conducting a real-world study with the Pepper humanoid robot with patients, doctors and nurses at UKCM hospital, integrating the robot into clinical environments.

PALANTIR (H2020)

As part of my PhD research, I was involved in the development of an incident management system for potential security threats. My contributions included:

  • Designing an incident management component based on simple finite state machines (FSMs) using the Spring State Machine framework in Java.
  • Implementing synchronous data transfer with Apache Camel (REST API) and asynchronous data transfer with Apache Kafka.
  • Testing AI-driven microservices using machine learning methods for threat detection in SMEs and medical environments.

SMILE (Horizon) & AI4HOPE (Horizon)

My work in these projects focused on FHIR server architecture expansion, data automation, and platform security:

  • FHIR & Data Automation: Enhancing the FHIR server architecture and automating FHIR data flows.
  • Security & Scalability: Implementing Keycloak-based authentication, VPN, and firewall mechanisms, while ensuring platform scalability for up to 5,000 simultaneous users.
  • Infrastructure & Development: Managing hardware, VMs, and containers, and continuously updating Python and Java-based microservices to improve digital health solutions.
